(NASDAQ:WFM) today reported results for the 16-week first quarter ended January&#160;15, 2017.&#160; For the quarter, total sales increased 1.9% to a record $4.9 billion.&#160; Comparable store sales decreased 2.4%.&#160; Net income was $95 million, or 1.9% of sales; diluted earnings per share were $0.30; and earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization (&#8220;EBITDA&#8221;) were $360 million, or 7.3% of sales.&#160; Results included a non-cash charge of $34 million, or $0.06 per diluted share, related to store and facility closures and a charge of $13 million, or $0.03 per diluted share, associated with Mr. Robb&#8217;s separation agreement.&#160; Excluding these charges, net income was $123 million, or 2.5% of sales; diluted earnings per share were $0.39; EBITDA margin was 7.6%; and return on invested capital was 12%.&#160; The Company expects to incur an additional charge in the second quarter of approximately $30 million, or $0.06 per diluted share, related to these closures.&#160; Please refer to the reconciliation of GAAP measures to non-GAAP measures at the end of this release.<br></p>  <p align="justify">During the quarter, the Company produced $284 million in cash flow from operations, invested $245 million in capital expenditures, and returned $43 million in quarterly dividends to shareholders.&#160; The Company ended the quarter with $1.1 billion of total debt and $1.2 billion of total available capital.</p>  <p align="justify">&#8220;In this increasingly competitive marketplace, we are committed to taking every step necessary to improve comps and deliver higher returns for our shareholders,&#8221; said John Mackey, co-founder and chief executive officer of Whole Foods Market. &#8220;To this end, we are refining our growth strategy, refocusing our efforts on best serving our core customers, and moving faster to fully implement category management. Evolving our purchasing operating model while developing data-rich, customer-centric category management capabilities is critical to our go-forward merchandising, pricing, marketing and affinity strategies."</p>  <p align="justify">The following table provides information on the Company&#8217;s comparable store sales trends for the first quarter and for the first three weeks of the 12-week second quarter.&#160; A 42 basis point improvement in wages was more than offset by higher marketing and depreciation expenses as a percentage of sales.</p>  <p align="justify"><strong>Growth and Development</strong><br>In the first quarter, the Company opened 13 stores, including two relocations.&#160; So far in the second quarter, the Company has opened three stores, including one relocation, and expects to open three additional stores, including one relocation. The Company also closed one commissary kitchen and will be closing&#160;nine stores and the Company&#8217;s last two remaining commissary kitchens during the quarter.&#160; The Company recently terminated two leases and signed four new leases and currently has 93 stores in development.</p>  <p align="justify"><strong>Fiscal Year 2017 Updated Outlook</strong><br>The Company is updating its outlook primarily to reflect lower expected sales growth and new costs associated with accelerating the implementation of category management.&#160; In the first quarter, the Company incurred a charge of approximately $47 million, or $0.09 per diluted share, related to Mr. Robb&#8217;s separation agreement&#160;as well as store and facility closures.&#160; In the second quarter, the Company expects to incur an additional charge related to these closures of approximately $30 million, or $0.06 per diluted share.&#160; The Company&#8217;s outlook excludes these charges and potential share repurchases.</p>  <p align="justify">The Company remains focused on the metrics it believes are key to the long-term health of its business and is targeting:</p>  <ul type="disc"><li>Sales growth of 1.5% or greater</li><li>Comps of approximately -2.5% or better</li><li>Ending square footage growth of approximately 5% net of closures, reflecting approximately 30 new stores, including up to six relocations and three 365 stores</li><li>Diluted EPS of $1.33 or greater</li><li>EBITDA margin of approximately 8%</li><li>Capital expenditures of approximately 4% of sales</li><li>ROIC of approximately 11%</li></ul>  <p align="justify">The Company&#160;has updated its sales outlook primarily to reflect year-to-date&#160;sales trends and lost sales related to the store closures.&#160; While the&#160;Company remains hopeful that comps improve as sales-building initiatives gain traction and comparisons get easier, the competitive landscape continues to be very dynamic, two-year comps have continued to moderate, and it is uncertain how long the deflationary environment will continue.</p>  <p align="justify">The Company plans to reduce its cost structure this fiscal year but expects these savings to be more than offset by investments in marketing, value and technology, as well as higher occupancy, depreciation and other costs.&#160;&#160;In&#160;addition, the Company is estimating additional costs of approximately $14 million, or $0.03 per diluted share,&#160;related to its recent decision to accelerate the implementation of category management, the majority of which it expects to incur in the fourth quarter.&#160; Therefore, the Company now expects a decline in operating margin of up to approximately 85 basis points for the year, with greater declines of up to 115 basis points in the second and fourth quarters due in part to the negative Easter shift and higher year-over-year marketing expense in the second quarter, and costs associated with category management in the fourth quarter.&#160; The Company also notes a LIFO credit of $9 million in the fourth quarter last year as compared to charges of $2 million in the first and second quarters and a credit of $2 million in the third quarter.</p>  <p align="justify"><strong>Seasonality</strong><br>Easter will fall in the third quarter of fiscal year 2017 versus the second quarter of fiscal year 2016, negatively impacting comparable store sales growth in the second quarter and positively impacting comparable store sales growth in the third quarter.&#160; Historically, the impact has been approximately 50 basis points.&#160; The Company notes that average weekly sales and gross profit as a percentage of sales are typically highest in the second and third fiscal quarters, and lowest in the fourth fiscal quarter due to seasonally slower sales during the summer months.&#160; Gross profit as a percentage of sales is also lower in the first fiscal quarter due to the product mix of holiday sales.</p>  <p align="justify"><strong>About Whole Foods Market</strong><br>Founded in 1978 in Austin, Texas, Whole Foods Market is the leading natural and organic foods supermarket, the first national &#8220;Certified Organic&#8221; grocer, and uniquely positioned as America&#8217;s Healthiest Grocery Store&#153;.&#160; In fiscal year 2016, the Company had sales of approximately $16 billion and currently has 469 stores in the United States, Canada, and the United Kingdom.&#160; Whole Foods Market employs approximately 87,000 team members and has been ranked for 19 consecutive years as one of the &#8220;100 Best Companies to Work For&#8221; in America by <em>Fortune </em>magazine.&#160;
