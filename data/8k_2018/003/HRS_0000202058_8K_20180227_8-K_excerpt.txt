Item 8.01 Other Events.

On February 27, 2018, Harris Corporation (“Harris”) closed its issuance and sale of $300,000,000 aggregate principal amount of its Floating Rate Notes due February 2019 (the “Notes”). The Notes will bear interest at a floating rate, reset quarterly, equal to three-month LIBOR plus 0.475% per year. 

The Notes were offered and sold under Harris’ “automatic shelf” Registration Statement on Form S-3 (Registration No. 333-213408) filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission (“SEC”) on August 31, 2016 (the “Registration Statement”). In connection with the issuance and sale of the Notes, Harris filed a Prospectus dated August 31, 2016, and a related Prospectus Supplement dated February 23, 2018, which relate to the issuance and sale of the Notes. 

The Notes were issued pursuant to an Indenture, dated as of September 3, 2003 (Exhibit 4(b)(i) to the Registration Statement), between Harris and The Bank of New York Mellon Trust Company, N.A., as successor to The Bank of New York, as trustee. 

The following documents relating to the sale of the Notes are filed as exhibits to this Current Report on Form 8-K and are incorporated into this Item 8.01 by reference: 

•Underwriting Agreement, dated as of February 23, 2018, between Harris and Citigroup Global Markets Inc., on behalf of itself and the several underwriters named therein;

•Form of Floating Rate Global Note due February 2019; and

•Opinion of Holland & Knight LLP as to the validity of the Notes.

Harris will use the net proceeds from the sale of the Notes, together with cash on hand, to make voluntary contributions of approximately $300 million to its U.S. qualified defined benefit pension plans during the remainder of its fiscal 2018.
